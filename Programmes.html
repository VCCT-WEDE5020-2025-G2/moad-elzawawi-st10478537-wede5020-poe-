Our Programmes

Comprehensive support services designed to empower children and strengthen communities

Making a Difference Through Action

Our programmes are designed to address the multi-faceted challenges facing underprivileged children in Cape Town. By tackling both immediate needs like hunger and long-term goals like education, we create a comprehensive support system that gives children the best chance to succeed.

Each programme is delivered through our network of 15 partner schools and is supported by over 200 dedicated volunteers and staff members who are committed to making a lasting difference in young lives.

Daily Feeding Schemes

Ensuring no child learns on an empty stomach

Our flagship programme provides nutritious meals to over 2,500 children daily across 15 partner schools. We understand that hungry children cannot concentrate on learning, which is why we prioritize food security as the foundation of educational success.

Programme Details:

  • Daily Meals: Breakfast and lunch provided Monday to Friday during school terms
  • Nutritional Standards: Meals designed by qualified nutritionists to meet children's dietary needs
  • Local Sourcing: We source ingredients from local suppliers to support the community economy
  • Holiday Support: Extended feeding during school holidays for vulnerable families
  • Take-Home Packages: Weekend food parcels for families in extreme need

Impact Metrics:

  • 2,500+ children fed daily
  • 15 partner schools
  • 95% improvement in school attendance rates
  • 400,000+ meals served annually

Cost Impact: R50 feeds a child for one week

After-School Tutoring Programme

Bridging educational gaps through personalized support

Our tutoring programme provides academic support to children who lack homework assistance at home. Qualified volunteers and teachers work with small groups to improve literacy, numeracy, and overall academic performance.

Programme Components:

  • Homework Support: Daily assistance with school assignments
  • Reading Programme: Literacy development for all age groups
  • Mathematics Support: Extra help with numeracy skills
  • Study Skills: Teaching effective learning techniques
  • Exam Preparation: Specialized support during exam periods
  • Digital Literacy: Basic computer skills training

Programme Schedule:

  • Time: Monday to Thursday, 2:00 PM - 5:00 PM
  • Group Size: Maximum 8 students per tutor
  • Age Groups: Grade 1-12 separated by age and ability
  • Language Support: Available in English, Afrikaans, and isiXhosa

Impact Results:

  • 500+ students receiving regular tutoring
  • 85% improvement in academic performance
  • 78% increase in Grade 12 pass rates
  • 150+ volunteer tutors

Community Events & Workshops

Building stronger communities through engagement and skills development

Our community events programme brings families and communities together while providing valuable skills training, health education, and recreational activities that strengthen social bonds and community resilience.

Regular Events:

  • Monthly Community Days: Family fun days with educational activities
  • Parent Workshops: Parenting skills, financial literacy, and job readiness training
  • Health Awareness Days: Free health screenings and wellness education
  • Skills Development Workshops: Basic computer literacy, entrepreneurship, and life skills
  • Cultural Celebrations: Heritage Day, Women's Day, and Youth Day events
  • Environmental Projects: Community garden maintenance and recycling initiatives

Special Annual Events:

  • Back-to-School Festival: Uniform and stationery distribution with entertainment
  • Winter Warmth Campaign: Blanket and warm clothing distribution
  • Christmas Giving: Toy and gift distribution for children
  • Education Awards Ceremony: Celebrating academic achievements
  • Community Sports Day: Inter-school competitions and family activities

Workshop Impact:

  • 2,000+ community members reached annually
  • 150+ parents completed skills training
  • 50+ families started small businesses after workshops
  • Monthly events with 300+ attendees

Educational Support & Resources

Removing barriers to education through resource provision

We believe that no child should miss school due to lack of uniforms, textbooks, or basic supplies. Our educational support programme ensures that financial constraints don't prevent children from accessing quality education.

Support Services:

  • Uniform Programme: School uniforms and shoes for children in need
  • Stationery Supplies: Books, pens, calculators, and other learning materials
  • Library Services: Mobile library visiting schools weekly
  • Technology Access: Computer and internet access for research and assignments
  • Transportation Support: Taxi vouchers for students living far from school
  • Scholarship Programme: Financial assistance for high-achieving students

Specialized Support:

  • Grade 12 Support: Intensive matric exam preparation
  • University Guidance: Application assistance and career counseling
  • Bursary Assistance: Help finding and applying for higher education funding
  • Career Mentorship: Connecting students with industry professionals

Programme Reach:

  • 800+ students receive uniforms annually
  • 1,200+ students get stationery supplies
  • 25 students on scholarship programme
  • 95% university application success rate

Support Education: R200 provides a full school uniform

Community Gardens & Nutrition Education

Teaching sustainable food production and healthy eating habits

Our community gardens programme teaches families how to grow their own food while providing fresh vegetables for our feeding schemes. This sustainable approach helps communities become more food secure while teaching valuable life skills.

Garden Projects:

  • School Gardens: Teaching children about nutrition and agriculture
  • Family Gardens: Home-based vegetable growing programmes
  • Community Plots: Shared growing spaces for families without yards
  • Herb Gardens: Medicinal and culinary herb cultivation
  • Composting Programme: Teaching waste reduction and soil improvement

Training Components:

  • Basic Gardening Skills: Soil preparation, planting, and maintenance
  • Nutrition Education: Understanding healthy eating and meal planning
  • Food Preservation: Techniques for storing and preserving harvests
  • Entrepreneurship: Starting small agricultural businesses

Environmental Impact:

  • 12 active community gardens
  • 300+ families growing their own food
  • 2,000kg of fresh vegetables produced monthly
  • 40% reduction in family food costs for participants
